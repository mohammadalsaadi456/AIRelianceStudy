Experiment Design

How we tested our framework with real students

The Controlled Experiment

Testing if our framework helps students use AI responsibly

10 Students

5 in controlled group, 5 in uncontrolled group

6 Tasks

Common school tasks that students use AI for

60 Minutes

Each student spent about an hour on the tasks

4 Measures

We measured quality, time, compliance, and mood

How We Did It

1

Recruited Students

We invited 10 Carleton University students to participate. All were between 18-25 years old and use AI for schoolwork.

2

Created Two Groups

We randomly split students into two groups:

  • Controlled Group: Received our AI framework before starting
  • Uncontrolled Group: Did not receive the framework
3

Gave Them Tasks

Each student completed 6 common school tasks. They could use AI tools like ChatGPT if they wanted to.

4

Collected Data

We recorded how they completed tasks, how long it took, the quality of their work, and how they felt about it.

5

Compared Results

We compared the two groups to see if students with our framework used AI more responsibly.

The 6 Tasks

Real school assignments students might use AI for

Task 1
10-15 min

Summarize an Article

Read a short academic article and write a summary in your own words.

Common AI Use: Asking ChatGPT to summarize
Task 2
5 min

Give Your Opinion

After summarizing, write your personal opinion about the article's topic.

Common AI Use: Getting help organizing thoughts
Task 3
10 min

Fix Grammar & Rephrase

Take a poorly written paragraph and improve the grammar and wording.

Common AI Use: Using Grammarly or ChatGPT editing
Task 4
10 min

Write a Short Story

Write a 2-paragraph story including specific characters and events.

Common AI Use: Getting story ideas or writing help
Task 5
5 min

Create a Study Schedule

Organize a list of tasks into a weekly schedule and explain your plan.

Common AI Use: Asking AI to create schedules
Task 6
5 min

Debug Simple Code

Find and fix errors in a basic HTML/CSS webpage.

Common AI Use: Using AI to find coding errors

We Asked About Feelings Too

Before and after each task, we asked students:

Before Task:

"How do you feel about this task?"

After Task:

"How do you feel now that you've finished?"

This helped us understand if using AI made students feel more or less confident, stressed, or satisfied with their work.
