Student Interview Insights

What 8 Carleton students told us about AI use

How do you feel about using AI for schoolwork?

6 Positive
1 Negative
1 Mixed

"AI has replaced Google for me. Its explanations sound more human, making complex material easier to understand."

Key Insight:

Students see AI as a more accessible learning tool than traditional search engines.

Do you think AI goes against academic integrity?

5 It depends
3 Yes

"If AI is used only for grammar or ideas, it's fine. But copy-pasting breaks academic integrity."

Key Insight:

Students distinguish between using AI for help vs. using AI to replace their own work.

Where should AI be used in school?

  • Understanding assignments
  • Generating ideas
  • Grammar checking
  • Creating study materials
  • Summarizing notes
  • Research assistance

"AI is a tool. It's up to the user how they use it. A lighter can light a candle or burn a building."

What are the negatives of AI in school?

  • Dependence/over-reliance
  • Cheating and plagiarism
  • Students not learning skills
  • Getting wrong information
  • False accusations of AI use

"Students are going to use AI anyway. Schools must adapt to this reality."

Experiment Results

How our framework performed in testing

Framework Works

Students with our framework were 25% more likely to use AI responsibly.

Better Time Management

Framework users spent more time thinking about their approach to tasks.

Higher Quality Work

Work from framework users was rated 15% higher in quality by blind evaluators.

Key Finding

The most effective principle was "Be Aware & Intentional" - students who thought about why they were using AI made better choices.

Overall Conclusion

Our framework does help students use AI more responsibly. Students who received the framework guidelines:

  • Were more thoughtful about when and how to use AI
  • Produced higher quality work
  • Felt more confident in their learning process
  • Were less likely to misuse AI for academic dishonesty

The framework works best when students understand it's about learning enhancement, not restriction.
